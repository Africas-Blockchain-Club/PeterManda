# Understanding Target 1.1 of SDG1: Eradicate Extreme Poverty

## Overview
Target 1.1 under Sustainable Development Goal 1 (SDG1) aims to eradicate extreme poverty for all people everywhere by 2030. Extreme poverty is currently defined as living on less than $2.15 a day (subject to periodic review). This README provides detailed insights into what this target entails and its significance.

## Target 1.1: Eradicate Extreme Poverty

**Key Points:**
- **Absolute Goal:** No one should be living below the poverty line by 2030.
- **Income Threshold:** The threshold is $2.15 per day, adjusted for purchasing power parity (PPP).
- **Universal Focus:** The target addresses extreme poverty in all its forms and dimensions globally.

## Significance of Target 1.1
- **Human Dignity:** Ensures basic needs like food, water, shelter, education, and healthcare.
- **Global Inequality:** Aims to reduce inequality and promote equal opportunities.
- **Economic Growth:** Increases participation in and contribution to the economy.
- **Social Stability:** Contributes to social stability and reduces conflict and instability.

## Obstacles
- **Measurement:** Accurately measuring poverty levels and understanding its multifaceted nature.
- **Economic Disparities:** Addressing root causes like economic disparities and lack of education.
- **Policy Implementation:** Ensuring effective policies and coordination at various levels.

## Assessment and Advancement
- **Poverty Line:** Monitoring the population percentage living below $2.15 per day (PPP adjusted).
- **Data Collection:** Using household surveys and other methods to track progress.
- **Progress Reports:** Regular reviews by international organisations like the UN and World Bank.
