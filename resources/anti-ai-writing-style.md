# Anti-AI Writing Style

**Read before writing or editing any content for Peter. These are the tells that get a post deleted.**

If a draft contains any of these, it reads like AI wrote it. Peter will delete the post over a single em dash. Hold this standard.

---

## 1. Punctuation Tells

- No em dashes. No en dashes. Ever. This is the single hardest rule. Use a hyphen or a semi-colon, or rewrite the sentence.
- No decorative ellipses trailing off for effect.
- No excessive bolding scattered through prose to fake emphasis.

---

## 2. Banned Filler Words and Phrases

Never use any of these:

- leverage
- dive into / let us dive in
- navigate (unless literal movement)
- unleash
- game-changer
- cutting-edge
- it is worth noting
- in today's landscape / in today's world
- let us explore
- delve
- elevate
- testament to
- in conclusion / to summarise (as a signposted close)
- "Great question" or any hollow opening affirmation

---

## 3. Structural Tells

- No generic AI intros that restate the prompt before answering.
- No transitional words between paragraphs: no "however", "furthermore", "in addition", "additionally", "moreover". Just start the next thought.
- No closing question designed to farm comments. No "What are your thoughts?" No "Drop a comment below."
- No call-to-action. No "follow me", no "DM me". The work speaks.
- No "I am proud to announce" or any variant.
- No certificate-announcement posts. Write about what the learning changed, not that you completed it.

---

## 4. Confidence Calibration

- Real experts acknowledge trade-offs, failures, and uncertainty. Anything that does not is performing.
- Never state a sweeping claim without naming the limitation, the failure rate, or the nuance.
- State conviction backed by personal experience as fact. Do not hedge it with "I think" or "in my opinion".
- Flag opinion only when the claim could be a generalisation or a stereotype.

---

## 5. Rhythm and Texture

- Short punchy sentences. Single-sentence paragraphs that stand alone.
- High signal per sentence. Cut rather than qualify.
- Protect rough edges. A post that is too smooth, too balanced, too polished reads as machine-made.
- No Latinate bombast: culminate, percolate, flabbergasted, pandemonium.
- No passive constructions that hide who did the thing.

---

## 6. The Reframe Standard

The Munroe move: name what people assume, then replace it with the real thing. Two sentences. State it plainly.

- Good: "That is not a technical gap. That is a confidence gap."
- Bad: "I think this might actually be more of a confidence issue than a technical one, if I am honest."

---

## 7. The Final Test

Before a post goes out, ask:

- Does it convict me when I read it back?
- Could a trusted source verify any factual claim, or is it personal story? If neither, do not post it.
- Does every sentence pass the conviction test, or is some of it padding?
- Would a human under real pressure have written this, or is it too perfect?

If it fails any of these, cut or rewrite. Do not publish to hit a deadline.
