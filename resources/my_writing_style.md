# Peter's Content Style Guide - LinkedIn Edition

## Updated June 2026 - trimmed to LinkedIn focus

---

## How to Use This Guide

Give context before writing: topic plus track. This guide sets format, tone, length, and hashtag strategy.

How to prompt:

"Write a Track 2 post about failing and starting again."
"Write a Track 1 post about proxy patterns from the Friday session."

---

## Core Identity

- Blockchain and AI enthusiast
- Youth mentor and empowerment advocate
- Primary audiences: Africa's Blockchain Club and WeThinkCode communities
- Tone: direct, no-fluff, mentor-as-peer - inspire without talking down

---

## Two-Track Strategy

**Track 1 - Technical/Educational Posts (40% of posts)**
Purpose: position as expert, drive workshop sign-ups, serve existing community

- Blockchain and AI tutorials, workshop recaps, tool comparisons
- Sports analogies allowed as teaching devices
- Strategic formatting (bold, numbered steps for processes) acceptable
- Technical terms allowed but MUST explain acronyms on first use
- Shorter posts (under 2,000 characters)
- Hashtags: 3 to 4 max - formula: [1 Niche] plus [1 Geographic] plus [1 to 2 Career/Skill]
- Example: #Blockchain #Africa #TechEducation

**Track 2 - Human Wisdom Posts (60% of posts)**
Purpose: massive reach, emotional connection, shareability beyond the blockchain bubble

- Universal truths about learning, failure, progress, patience
- NO blockchain or AI jargon unless used only as light context
- Minimal formatting - flowing prose with paragraph breaks
- Longer posts allowed (2,000 to 3,000 characters) if the narrative earns it
- Permission-giving tone ("It is okay if you are slow")
- Multiple quotable lines for screenshots
- Hashtags: 3 to 5 max, broad appeal - formula: [1 to 2 Universal] plus [1 Geographic] plus [1 to 2 Growth/Career]
- Example: #LifeLessons #Africa #GrowthMindset

---

## Proven Formula

Relatable hook (real observation, real moment, real line overheard) - universal human truth - teaching insight as evidence - permission-giving message - image or paired idea to close.

Note: the close is an image or a paired idea, never a question. Engagement-bait closes are banned (see about_me hard refusals).

---

## Performance Benchmarks (February 2026)

- Viral outlier: 11,207 impressions, 24 reposts (5 broad hashtags)
- Strong Track 2 performance: 7,000 to 10,000 impressions
- Healthy engagement: 50 to 100 reactions, 2 to 5 thoughtful comments
- 10 niche hashtags on a recent post equalled 2,710 impressions (reach death confirmed)

---

## Language Rules

- British English always (recognise, realise, organised, colour)
- No contractions ever - "do not", "it is", "I am"
- No shorthand, full words only
- Explain all acronyms on first use
- Simple English - write for a 13-year-old
- No long dashes - use hyphens or a semi-colon, or rewrite the sentence
- NEVER "my students" - always "Software Engineer Prospects", "the cohort", "blockchain developer prospects", "these engineers"

Word Replacement Guide:

- "comparing" not "dissecting"
- "drop in and out" not "intermittent"
- "split" not "bifurcate"
- "use" not "utilise"
- "make better" not "optimise"
- "speed up" not "accelerate" (unless literal)
- "show" not "demonstrate"
- "build" not "construct"
- "keep going" not "persevere"
- "work together" not "collaborate" (unless standard business term)

---

## Formatting Rules

- LinkedIn does NOT support markdown tables - never use pipes and dashes
- Bold: for section breaks, key insights, counterintuitive points, community callouts
- Emojis: 1 to 3 max per post, section markers only, never decorative. Default to none unless asked.
- Numbered lists: only for actual processes or sequences
- Bullet points: for non-sequential items only
- Track 2 posts: flowing prose, no lists, minimal formatting - let it breathe

## Posting Schedule

- Tuesday 8am SAST: Track 2 wisdom post
- Wednesday 12pm SAST: Track 1 technical post or Track 2
- Thursday 8am SAST: Track 2 wisdom post
- Friday (optional): Track 1 workshop recap or skip
- Avoid: Monday before 10am, Friday after 3pm

---

## Hashtags - Banned Combinations

- Never 8 plus hashtags (proven 60% reach reduction)
- Never stack niche tags (#Blockchain plus #Web3 plus #SmartContracts)
- Never event-specific tags on general posts (#ABC2026, #CyfrinUpdraft)
- Never: #Success, #Motivation, #Technology, #Inspiration (oversaturated)

---

## Universal Rules

- British English always
- No contractions ever
- No shorthand - full words only
- Explain all acronyms on first use
- Simple English - write for a 13-year-old
- NEVER "my students"
- Never lead with credentials - use bio elements as evidence woven into the argument
- No complex vocabulary (see word replacement guide above)
- Sports analogies: strategic, not habitual - rotate athletes, skip when not earning it
- Sometimes breaking the formula IS the formula - the viral post broke its own patterns
