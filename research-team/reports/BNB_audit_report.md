## Phase 1.5: Tokenomics & AMM Math

> **Why we look at this:** Most DEX tokens trade inside Automated Market Makers (AMMs). The AMM formula directly determines how much price moves when someone buys or sells. This section runs the actual maths so you know exactly what happens to price when capital enters or exits.

### 1.5.1 Liquidity Pool Equation

The most common AMM uses the Constant Product formula: x multiplied by y equals k, where x is the quantity of the base token in the pool, y is the quantity of the quote token (usually a stablecoin or paired asset), and k is a fixed number that never changes. When a buyer removes base tokens from the pool, the pool must increase the price to keep k constant. This is why large buys cause price to spike and large sells cause price to crash, especially in thin pools. In plain English: imagine a seesaw with BNB on one side and the quote token on the other. If you remove BNB from the pool, the other side gets heavier relative to BNB, which means each remaining BNB is worth more. The opposite happens when you sell BNB into the pool.

**Important context for this data:** The Raydium (Solana) pair being analysed here holds 999,713 BNB as base tokens and only 5.3848 units of the quote token. The quote token quantity of 5.3848 is anomalously small, which indicates this Raydium pair is not a standard BNB/USDC stablecoin pool. It is most likely a BNB/SOL or BNB/wrapped-asset pair where the quote token is a high-value asset (such as SOL or a large stablecoin denomination). The $721.47M liquidity figure from DexScreener reflects aggregated dollar-denominated depth across all pairs, not this single pool in isolation. The AMM maths below uses the raw data as provided.

| Variable | Value |
|----------|-------|
| Base Token Qty (BNB in pool) | 999,713 BNB |
| Quote Token Qty (in pool) | 5.3848 units |
| Constant Product k = Base x Quote | 999,713 x 5.3848 = 5,383,248.77 |
| Current Price (implied by pool ratio) | 999,713 / 5.3848 = ~185,665 BNB per quote unit (confirms quote is not USDC; likely SOL or similar high-denomination asset) |
| Estimated price after 10% base removed | New base = 999,713 - 99,971.3 = 899,741.7; New quote = 5,383,248.77 / 899,741.7 = 5.9831 units |
| Estimated price impact of 10% removal | Quote increases from 5.3848 to 5.9831 = +11.12% price impact |

Calculation shown explicitly: 999,713 x 5.3848 = 5,383,248.77 (k). After removing 10% of base (99,971.3 BNB): 899,741.7 x new_quote = 5,383,248.77. Therefore new_quote = 5,383,248.77 / 899,741.7 = 5.9831. Price impact = (5.9831 - 5.3848) / 5.3848 = 0.1112 = 11.12%.

> **Reading this table:** A 10% removal of base tokens from this specific Raydium pool produces an 11.12% price impact on the pool's internal pricing. However, this specific Raydium pair should not be used as the primary trading venue for BNB at scale. The quote token quantity of 5.3848 confirms this is not a deep stablecoin pool. BNB's primary liquidity at scale resides on Binance CEX and other centralised exchanges. The $721.47M DEX liquidity figure is the more representative number for market structure analysis.

## Red Flag Kill Switch Assessment

> **Why kill switches exist:** Even a token with a high Blueprint Score can become a trap if one of these structural red flags is present. These flags represent conditions where the downside risk is so severe and so immediate that no score justifies holding the token. If any of the first four are triggered, the Blueprint Score is overridden to below 20 regardless of other findings.

Before the table: kill switch flags require plain-English context so that any reader understands the practical risk.

- **Thin Order Books:** If you could crash the price with a relatively small sell order, large holders (whales) can exit cleanly while retail traders cannot. It means the price you see is not the price you will get.
- **Supply Influx:** When large numbers of locked tokens unlock all at once, early investors and team members who bought at a fraction of the current price are highly incentivised to sell immediately. This creates a predictable crash.
- **Concentration Risk:** If ten wallets control more than 80% of supply, a single phone call between those holders can move more capital than all retail traders combined. The market is effectively not decentralised.
- **Artificial Volume:** If a token appears to trade more than its entire market cap in a single day, the volume is almost certainly fake. Fake volume is used to attract retail buyers into a manipulated market.
