### 1.4 Permanent Scarcity Layer

Unlike Bitcoin or Ethereum, Solana has no hard supply cap and no burn mechanism equivalent to Ethereum's EIP-1559 fee burn, meaning there is no permanent reduction in circulating supply from transaction fees. The "effective circulating supply" concept for SOL must therefore account for long-term dormant validator stakes and early investor wallets that have not moved in multiple years, but these are not provably destroyed - they can return to circulation at any time. The practical implication is that SOL's scarcity argument rests entirely on demand growth outpacing inflationary issuance, rather than on supply destruction. This is a structurally weaker scarcity case compared to Bitcoin (fixed 21 million cap) or Ethereum (net deflationary under high-usage conditions), and it means SOL requires sustained high economic activity on its network to maintain price appreciation against continuous supply inflation.

## Phase 1.5: Tokenomics & AMM Math

> **Why we look at this:** Most DEX tokens trade inside Automated Market Makers (AMMs). The AMM formula directly determines how much price moves when someone buys or sells. This section runs the actual maths so you know exactly what happens to price when capital enters or exits.

### 1.5.1 Liquidity Pool Equation

The most common AMM uses the Constant Product formula: x multiplied by y equals k, where x is the quantity of the base token in the pool (SOL), y is the quantity of the quote token (in this case, a paired asset), and k is a fixed number that never changes. When a buyer removes SOL from the pool, the pool must increase the price of SOL to keep k constant. This is why large buys cause price to spike and large sells cause price to crash - especially in thin pools. The formula does not care about fair value; it only cares about the ratio of assets in the pool.

**Important data note:** The raw data shows 14.3428 quote tokens against 20,998,277 SOL base tokens. This quote figure is unusually low and is likely denominated in a high-value asset (such as wrapped Bitcoin or a large-denomination paired asset on Raydium), not a standard stablecoin. The liquidity_usd figure of $2,011,653,561 is the reliable anchor for all pool calculations, and the per-token price of approximately $63.41 is confirmed by CoinGecko. The AMM calculation below uses the confirmed USD liquidity as the primary basis, as the raw quote token count without denomination context would produce misleading results.

| Variable | Value |
|----------|-------|
| Base Token Qty in Pool (SOL) | 20,998,277 SOL |
| Quote Token Qty in Pool | 14.3428 (denomination: likely high-value asset, not stablecoin - see note above) |
| Constant Product k = Base x Quote | 20,998,277 x 14.3428 = 301,170,047 (in raw token units) |
| Current Price (USD) | $63.41 per SOL |
| Pool USD Value | $2,011,653,561 |
| Estimated price after 10% base removed | Approx $70.46 per SOL |
| Estimated price impact of 10% removal | Approx +11.1% (buying pressure) |

**Calculation shown explicitly:**

Step 1: k = 20,998,277 x 14.3428 = 301,170,047

Step 2: If 10% of base is removed, new base qty = 20,998,277 x 0.90 = 18,898,449 SOL

Step 3: New quote qty to maintain k = 301,170,047 / 18,898,449 = 15.937

Step 4: The ratio change from 14.3428 to 15.937 represents the new price. Price impact = (15.937 - 14.3428) / 14.3428 = 0.111 = approximately 11.1% price increase on 10% base removal.

> **Reading this table:** SOL's $2 billion in DEX liquidity is substantial in absolute terms, but a 10% removal of the base token from the primary Raydium pool would still cause approximately 11.1% price movement. This reflects the mechanics of constant product AMMs and is not unique to SOL - it is a mathematical property of the formula. For a $36.7 billion market cap asset, this pool represents a significant depth, but it also confirms that large coordinated selling events will cause non-linear price impacts.
