Unlike Bitcoin or Ethereum post-merge, Solana does not have a provable burn mechanism or a hard supply cap that creates permanent scarcity. Effective circulating supply is therefore broadly equal to the stated 578,559,136 SOL, minus an estimated 1-3% of total supply in wallets that have been dormant for over five years (primarily lost genesis wallets and abandoned early accounts) - this reduces the realistic effective supply to approximately 560-570 million SOL. Solana's inflation schedule is designed to decrease over time, targeting a long-run rate of 1.5% annually, which partially mitigates the continuous emissions. Effective scarcity for SOL is therefore not a supply-destruction story but a demand-growth story: price appreciation requires new capital inflows to exceed the dilution from ongoing staking emissions, which at current market cap means approximately $1.72B per year in new demand is required just to hold price flat against inflation.

## Phase 1.5: Tokenomics & AMM Math

> **Why we look at this:** Most DEX tokens trade inside Automated Market Makers (AMMs - a type of DEX that uses a mathematical formula to price assets instead of an order book). The AMM formula directly determines how much price moves when someone buys or sells. This section runs the actual maths so you know exactly what happens to price when capital enters or exits.

### 1.5.1 Liquidity Pool Equation

The Constant Product formula works like this: imagine a pool with two buckets of assets. The rule is that the product (multiplication) of what is in both buckets must always stay the same. If someone takes tokens out of bucket one (base token), they must put enough of bucket two (quote token) in to keep the product constant. Because you need to add progressively more of the quote token to get the same amount of base token, the price rises as you buy. This is why big buys spike price and big sells crash it - the pool is rebalancing to maintain its fixed constant.

For SOL, the data shows an unusual structure: the liquidity pool on Raydium has 20,998,277 SOL as the base token but only 14.3428 units of the quote token. This is almost certainly reporting SOL paired against a non-stablecoin asset (likely SOL/WSOL or another SOL-denominated pair) rather than a USD stablecoin pair, which explains the extremely small quote number. The $2.01B liquidity figure is the correct total liquidity value. The raw AMM calculation is shown below for transparency.

| Variable | Value |
|----------|-------|
| Base Token Qty (SOL in pool) | 20,998,277 SOL |
| Quote Token Qty (in pool) | 14.3428 units |
| Constant Product k = Base x Quote | 20,998,277 x 14.3428 = 301,182,041 |
| Current Implied Price | 20,998,277 / 14.3428 = 1,464,017 (base/quote units - this confirms the quote is not a USD stablecoin) |
| Estimated price after 10% base removed | New base = 18,898,449; New quote = 301,182,041 / 18,898,449 = 15.9367 units |
| Estimated price impact of 10% removal | (15.9367 - 14.3428) / 14.3428 = 11.11% price increase in quote-token terms |

**Calculation shown explicitly:** 20,998,277 x 14.3428 = 301,182,041 (k constant)

After 10% base removal: 20,998,277 x 0.90 = 18,898,449 (new base). Then 301,182,041 / 18,898,449 = 15.937 (new quote). Price impact = (15.937 - 14.3428) / 14.3428 = 11.11%.

> **Reading this table:** The raw pool ratio data from DexScreener reflects a non-stablecoin pair, which is why the implied price does not match SOL's market price of $63.19. The $2.01B total liquidity figure is the operative number for market structure analysis. For context: SOL's $2.01B in DEX liquidity against a $36.5B market cap represents a Depth-to-MCap ratio of 5.5%, which is substantially healthier than most altcoins. The 11.11% price impact from a 10% pool drain is high but less meaningful given that the $2.01B depth means a 10% drain would require a $201M trade.

### 2.3 Cost Basis Analysis

STH (Short-Term Holder) cost basis refers to the average acquisition price of wallets that bought SOL within the last 155 days. With SOL at $63.19 and the 30-day change at -29.10%, any wallet that purchased SOL from approximately September 2025 onward is likely underwater (holding at a loss relative to their purchase price). When STH holders are underwater, they create resistance at every price recovery because they are waiting to "break even" before selling - this dynamic converts what would otherwise be clean rallies into stalling recoveries. Given that the Yahoo Finance headline confirms $88M in long liquidations occurred during the recent flush to 2.5-year lows, a significant portion of leveraged STH positions have already been wiped out, which paradoxically removes some of the resistance overhead. The remaining concern is the cohort of spot buyers who purchased between $89-$120 who have not yet capitulated - this group represents a probable supply overhang for any recovery attempt.
