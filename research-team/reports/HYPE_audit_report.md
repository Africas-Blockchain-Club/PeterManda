## Phase 1.5: Tokenomics & AMM Math

> **Why we look at this:** Most DEX tokens trade inside Automated Market Makers (AMMs). The AMM formula directly determines how much price moves when someone buys or sells. This section runs the actual maths so you know exactly what happens to price when capital enters or exits.

### 1.5.1 Liquidity Pool Equation

The most common AMM uses the Constant Product formula: x multiplied by y equals k, where x is the quantity of the base token (HYPE) in the pool, y is the quantity of the quote token (in this case an extremely small fractional amount, suggesting this Uniswap pair is not the primary trading venue for HYPE), and k is a fixed number that never changes. When a buyer removes base tokens from the pool, the pool must increase the price to keep k constant. This is why large buys cause price to spike and large sells cause price to crash, especially in thin pools.

**Important data note:** The DexScreener data shows the Uniswap pair (0x2f8FDD5E52f690c42b4A5F4A04E1285b1e35c22a) has 49,999,999 HYPE as the base token and only 0.00009657 of the quote token. This quote token quantity is effectively zero relative to the base, which indicates this specific Uniswap pair is either: (a) a one-sided liquidity position used for bridging/reference purposes, not active trading; or (b) a pair where the quote token is a very high-denomination asset (such as a wrapped BTC or similarly valued asset). The 24h DEX pair volume of $0.16 confirms this pair is not where HYPE price discovery happens. The $2,826,705,964 liquidity figure reported appears to represent the total cross-platform liquidity for HYPE, not this single Uniswap pair. Price discovery for HYPE occurs on the Hyperliquid L1 native exchange itself.
